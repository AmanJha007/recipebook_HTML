Vada Pav

Vada Pav is a popular Indian street food consisting of a spicy potato fritter (vada) sandwiched between a soft bread roll (pav).
A delicious and satisfying snack that combines the crispy, flavorful vada with the soft, slightly sweet pav.
It's a beloved comfort food enjoyed by people of all ages across India.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • Water (as needed for batter)
  • Oil (for deep frying)
  • 4 pav buns
  • Green chutney and tamarind chutney (for serving)

Steps to follow for cooking

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, mustard seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, and chopped coriander leaves. Mix well and shape the mixture into small patties.
  2. In another bowl, prepare the batter by mixing chickpea flour with water to achieve a smooth consistency. Season with a pinch of salt.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Dip each potato patty into the chickpea batter, ensuring it is fully coated, and then carefully drop it into the hot oil. Fry until the vadas are golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. To assemble the vada pav, slice the pav buns horizontally without cutting all the way through. Place a fried vada inside each bun and serve with green chutney and tamarind chutney on the side.

Your delicious vada pav is ready enjoy and devour it with your family members
