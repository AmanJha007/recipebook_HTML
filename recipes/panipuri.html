Pani Puri

Pani Puri is a popular Indian street food consisting of crispy, hollow puris filled with spicy tamarind water and various toppings.
A burst of flavor in a single bite. These crispy, hollow, fried dough balls are stuffed with spicy potatoes and filled with tangy, minty, and spicy tamarind water.
It's eaten around all the parts of our country

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed for dough)
  • Oil (for deep frying)
  • 2 large potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon black salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Water (as needed for the tamarind water)

Steps to follow for cooking

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a stiff dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into thin circles. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the circles until they puff up and turn golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  3. In another bowl, mix the mashed potatoes with chaat masala, red chili powder, cumin powder, and salt. Set aside.
  4. For the tamarind water, blend tamarind pulp, mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, roasted cumin powder, black salt, sugar, and water until smooth. Strain the mixture to remove solids.
  5. To serve, make a small hole in the center of each puri, fill it with the spiced potato mixture, and dip it into the tamarind water before eating.

Your pani puri is ready enjoy and devour it with your family members
