ROTI

Roti, also known as chapati, is a staple Indian flatbread made from whole wheat flour.
It is a simple yet essential part of Indian cuisine, often served alongside curries and vegetables.
Roti is soft, pliable, and has a slightly nutty flavor that complements a wide variety of dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (optional, for softer rotis)

Steps to follow

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water while mixing to form a soft dough. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  2. After resting, divide the dough into equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each ball into a thin circle using a rolling pin on a lightly floured surface.
  3. Heat a tawa or flat griddle over medium-high heat. Place the rolled-out roti on the hot tawa. Cook for about 30 seconds until you see bubbles forming on the surface, then flip it over.
  4. Cook the other side for another 30 seconds, then flip again. Press gently with a spatula or cloth to help it puff up. Cook until both sides have golden brown spots and the roti is cooked through.
  5. Remove from the tawa and brush with oil or ghee if desired. Serve hot with your favorite curry or vegetable dish.
